# Product Analysis

## Purpose

Use this skill to understand the product, its commercial value, feature set, buyer motivation, and content angles before writing any product page, landing page, comparison, FAQ, or blog content.

## Source Basis

Consolidated from product-manager, market-researcher, business-analyst, content-marketer, and competitive-analyst skills.

## Required Analysis

- Product name and category.
- Product type: WordPress plugin, WooCommerce extension, LearnPress add-on, SaaS, Shopify app, or other.
- Core promise in one sentence.
- Primary business goal.
- Main customer problem.
- Current alternatives.
- Key features.
- Key benefits.
- Differentiators.
- Proof points available.
- Pricing model and purchase friction.
- Risks, limitations, or claims that require validation.

## Product Content Rules

- Benefits must be tied to features.
- Do not write vague claims like "powerful", "seamless", or "all-in-one" without proof.
- Do not invent metrics, reviews, ratings, active installs, compatibility, or customer evidence.
- Mark unsupported claims as `Assumption` or `Cần validate`.
- Product content must help a buyer decide whether this product fits their use case.

## Output Checklist

- Product summary.
- Buyer problem.
- Buyer motivation.
- Top use cases.
- Feature-to-benefit table.
- Differentiation table.
- Messaging priorities.
- Open questions.
